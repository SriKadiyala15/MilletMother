Tips & Storage

Tips for Using Millets

  • Soak millets for 6-8 hours or overnight to reduce cooking time and enhance nutrient absorption.
  • Always rinse millets thoroughly under running water before cooking to remove impurities.
  • Use the right water-to-millet ratio (usually 2:1 or 2.5:1) for the best texture.
  • Lightly toast millets in a dry pan before cooking to enhance their nutty flavor.
  • Use millets as a substitute for rice, wheat, or oats in various recipes.
  • Pair millets with vegetables, herbs, and spices to enhance flavor and nutrition.


Millet Storage Information

  • Store millets in an airtight container to prevent moisture and pest infestation.
  • Keep in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
  • Refrigerate or freeze millets for longer shelf life, especially in humid climates.
  • Check for unusual odor, discoloration, or insects before cooking.
  • Use clean, dry spoons to scoop millets and avoid contamination.
  • Label containers with the purchase date to use older stock first.


Shelf Life of Millets

Uncooked Millets:

  • Room Temperature: 6-8 months
  • Refrigerated: Up to 1 year
  • Frozen: Up to 2 years

Cooked Millets:

  • Refrigerator: 3-5 days
  • Freezer: Up to 3 months


Common Issues & Solutions

  • Pest Infestation: Add bay leaves or neem leaves to millet containers.
  • Moisture Issues: Place silica gel packets (food-safe) or rice grains in the container.
  • Clumping of Cooked Millets: Add a few drops of oil or fluff with a fork after cooking.
