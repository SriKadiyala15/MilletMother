Types of Millets

Finger Millet (Ragi)

Description: Known as a powerhouse of nutrients, Ragi is rich in calcium and dietary fiber, making it an excellent choice for bone health and digestion.

Health Benefits:

  • Helps manage diabetes
  • Boosts bone strength
  • Aids in weight loss

Popular Dishes: Ragi porridge, Ragi roti, Ragi malt.

Pearl Millet (Bajra)

Description: A widely cultivated millet, Pearl Millet is packed with iron, magnesium, and protein.

Health Benefits:

  • Improves heart health
  • Reduces bad cholesterol levels
  • Enhances digestion

Popular Dishes: Bajra khichdi, Bajra roti, Bajra ladoo.

Foxtail Millet

Description: A small, easily digestible grain, Foxtail Millet is high in complex carbohydrates and low in fat.

  • Helps control blood sugar levels
  • Boosts immunity
  • Improves skin and hair health

Popular Dishes: Foxtail millet upma, dosa, and salad.

Sorghum (Jowar)

Description: One of the most versatile millets, Jowar is gluten-free and loaded with antioxidants, iron, and protein.

Health Benefits:

  • Promotes heart health.
  • Improves bone density.
  • Provides energy and boosts metabolism.

Popular Dishes: Jowar roti, Jowar dosa, Jowar porridge.

Barnyard Millet

Description: This millet is low in calories and high in fiber, making it an excellent choice for weight watchers.

Health Benefits:

  • Helps in weight management.
  • Reduces cholesterol.
  • Good for digestion.

Popular Dishes: Barnyard millet pongal, Barnyard millet khichdi.

Little Millet

Description: Known for its small grain size, Little Millet is rich in iron and fiber, and it cooks quickly, making it perfect for busy schedules.

Health Benefits:

  • Controls blood sugar levels.
  • Improves gut health.
  • Aids in detoxification.

Popular Dishes: Little millet pulao, Little millet idli.

Kodo Millet

Description: Kodo Millet is a rich source of polyphenols, antioxidants, and dietary fiber, making it beneficial for overall health.

Health Benefits:

  • Aids in weight management.
  • Detoxifies the body.
  • Reduces joint pain and inflammation.

Popular Dishes: Kodo millet biryani, Kodo millet salad.

Proso Millet

Description: A nutrient-dense grain, Proso Millet is high in protein and low in glycemic index, making it ideal for diabetic-friendly diets.

Health Benefits:

  • Improves digestion.
  • Helps manage diabetes.
  • Boosts energy levels.

Popular Dishes: Proso millet soup, Proso millet kheer.

Brown Top Millet

Description: A lesser-known millet, Brown Top Millet is highly nutritious and helps rejuvenate the body with its rich nutrient profile.

Health Benefits:

  • Enhances digestion.
  • Reduces cholesterol.
  • Improves heart health.

Popular Dishes: Brown top millet upma, Brown top millet dosa.
